# Wood Stud Wall Framing Requirements — CRC 2025 R602 (California Residential Code, Title 24 Part 2.5)

> Source: California Residential Code 2025, Chapter 6 (Wall Construction), via UpCodes public viewer.
> Retrieved 2026-06-06. For RAG ingestion — each section below is a self-contained chunk with citation.

## R602.3 — Design and Construction (general rule)

Exterior walls of wood-frame construction shall be designed and constructed in accordance with the provisions of Chapter 6 and must comply with specified fastening schedules and sheathing requirements.

Studs shall be continuous from support at the sole plate to a support at the top plate to resist loads perpendicular to the wall.

Citation: CRC 2025 §R602.3

---

## Table R602.3(5) — Stud Size, Height and Spacing (THE stud spacing table)

### Bearing walls (supporting roof, floors, or both)

| Stud size | Max spacing | Condition | Max unsupported height |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2×4 | **24 inches on center** | supporting roof-ceiling assembly only | 14 ft |
| 2×4 | **16 inches on center** | supporting one floor + roof-ceiling | 14 ft |
| 2×6 | **24 inches on center** | most load conditions | 20 ft |
| 3×4 and larger | greater spans/heights permitted | — | — |

### Nonbearing walls

- Studs may be spaced up to **24 inches on center**
- 2×3 studs are permitted for **interior nonbearing walls only** (not exterior)
- Maximum unsupported heights: 10–14 ft depending on stud size

**Practical rule of thumb: standard wall framing in the US is 16" on center (16 OC); 24" OC is allowed in specific lower-load conditions. Any measured spacing greater than the applicable maximum (e.g., 19" where 16" OC is required) is a code violation and will fail inspection.**

Citation: CRC 2025 Table R602.3(5)

---

## R602.2 — Grade (stud lumber quality)

Studs shall be a minimum No. 3, standard or stud grade lumber. Exception: utility grade studs are allowed where spacing does not exceed 16 inches on center for nonbearing applications.

Citation: CRC 2025 §R602.2

## Demo Q&A mapping (for index sanity check)

- Q: "What's the required stud spacing for interior walls?" → A: 16" or 24" on center per Table R602.3(5), depending on load condition; standard practice is 16 OC.
- Q: "I measured 19 inches between studs, is that OK?" → A: No — 19" exceeds both the 16" OC requirement for bearing walls supporting a floor and is not a recognized spacing; inspector will reject. Cite CRC 2025 Table R602.3(5).
- Q: "Can I use 2x3 studs?" → A: Only for interior nonbearing walls. Cite CRC 2025 Table R602.3(5).
